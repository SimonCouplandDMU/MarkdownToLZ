Example

Quadratic Formula

To solve a quadratic equation:

\[ ax^2 + bx + c = 0 \]

Use the formula:

\[ x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a} \]

Where: - \(a\), \(b\), and \(c\) are constants - \(\sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}\) is called the discriminant
